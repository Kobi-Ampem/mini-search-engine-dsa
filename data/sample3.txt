Data Structures are specialized formats for organizing and storing data in a computer. 
They provide a means to manage large amounts of data efficiently. Common data structures 
include arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, and graphs. Arrays are collections 
of elements stored at contiguous locations. Linked lists consist of nodes connected by pointers. 
Stacks follow a Last-In-First-Out principle. Queues follow a First-In-First-Out principle. 
Trees are hierarchical data structures. Graphs consist of vertices and edges. The choice of 
data structure affects the performance of algorithms. Efficient data structures are essential 
for writing efficient programs.
